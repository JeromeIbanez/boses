A **simulation** is a research study run against a persona group. Boses supports six simulation types, each modelling a different qualitative or quantitative research method.

---

## Simulation lifecycle

```
pending → running → complete
                 → failed
```

All simulations run as background tasks. After creating one, poll `GET /simulations/{id}` for status updates. The `progress` field shows live per-persona progress during long runs.

---

## Simulation types

### Concept Test
The most common starting point. Each persona reads your prompt and responds with:
- A **reaction** (in their own voice)
- A **sentiment** (Positive / Neutral / Negative)
- **Key themes** they noticed
- A **notable quote** you can use in presentations

The aggregate result includes a sentiment distribution, top themes across the group, and strategic recommendations.

### Survey
Structured questionnaire. You define questions in a `survey_schema` and each persona answers every question independently. Good for quantifiable preference data.

### Focus Group
Personas interact as a group in multiple rounds. Captures emergent consensus, disagreement, and social dynamics that individual interviews miss.

### In-Depth Interview (AI)
A free-form one-on-one exploration. The AI moderates the conversation, probing for depth. Best for uncovering unarticulated needs or emotional responses.

### In-Depth Interview (Manual)
You drive the conversation with a specific persona. Use this when you want to explore a hypothesis or follow an unexpected thread from a previous simulation.

### Conjoint Analysis
Attribute trade-off analysis. Personas evaluate product configurations and reveal which features drive choice — and at what price point.

---

## Result structure

Every simulation produces:
- **Per-persona results** — individual reactions, sentiments, quotes
- **Aggregate result** — synthesised themes, sentiment distribution, recommendations

Access them at `GET /simulations/{id}/results`.

---

## Tips

<Tip>
Run a concept test first. It's the fastest way to get a directional read. Use focus groups or IDIs to go deeper on specific findings.
</Tip>

<Tip>
The richer your `prompt_question`, the richer the persona responses. Include price points, visuals descriptions, and competitive context.
</Tip>
